8 Promotion — A Gap to Close
ALERT Vatika currently has ZERO promotional activity in Iraq. The market leader TRICHUP (Indian) runs 74% of its volume through promotions. Even P&G (HEAD&SHOULDERS, PANTENE) activates tactical promos at 0.5–1.6%. Vatika needs baseline promotional presence to drive trial in new distribution outlets.
9 Strategic Recommendations

1. Crack Traditional Trade

Traditional Trade (37% of market) is nearly unserved (WD 1.2). Deploy dedicated GT sales force in Baghdad and South. Target WD 10 within 12 months. Addressable value: ~2.2 Bn IQD annually.

Impact: +1.5% value share

2. Unlock Kurdistan

Kurdistan (843 K LTs) has Vatika WD of just 3.6 vs 22.4 in Baghdad. Appoint a regional distributor with Perfumery + Modern Trade coverage. This single region could add ~500 Mn IQD annually.

Impact: +0.4% value share

3. Launch Trial Promotions

Zero promo activity vs 74% by TRICHUP. Introduce shelf-displays + sampling in top 200 Perfumery outlets and 50 Modern Trade stores. Budget: modest — aimed at trial conversion in newly-distributed stores.

Impact: 2–3x velocity uplift in new outlets

4. Explore Premium Tier Entry

Premium tier (11–15K IQD PPV) holds 32% of market value (~137 Bn IQD) with no Dabur presence. A Vatika Premium/Keratin line at 12K IQD PPV could carve 1–2% tier share = ~1.4 Bn IQD.

Impact: Portfolio diversification + margin uplift

5. Strengthen South Region

South (1,094 K LTs — 23% of market) has Vatika WD of only 8.3 vs 22.4 Baghdad. This is the second-largest region with clear headroom. Prioritise distributor expansion alongside Baghdad playbook.

Impact: +0.5% value share from distribution parity
Bottom line: Vatika's brand pull is strong (efficiency 13.0) and pricing is well-positioned in Mainstream. The single biggest lever is distribution expansion — particularly Traditional Trade (nearly absent) and Kurdistan (3.6 WD). Executing recommendations 1–3 alone could take Vatika from 1.3% → 3%+ value share within 12–18 months, translating to an incremental ~6 Bn IQD in annual revenue.

Footnotes & Definitions

Key Metrics

[1]PPV (Price Per Volume) — Total Value (IQD) ÷ Total Volume (Litres). Indicates average price per litre charged in market. Higher PPV = premium positioning. Formula: Val Mn IQD × 1,000,000 ÷ Vol K LTs × 1,000.
[2]PPU (Price Per Unit) — Total Value (IQD) ÷ Total Packs sold. Indicates average shelf price per pack/bottle. Not always available where pack-level data is missing (shown as "—").
[3]WD (Weighted Distribution) — Percentage of total market value sold through stores that stock the brand. A brand with WD 50 is available in stores representing 50% of category sales. Higher WD = better reach in high-value outlets.
[4]ND (Numeric Distribution) — Percentage of total stores that stock the brand, regardless of store size. Measures breadth of physical availability.
[5]Distribution Efficiency — Value Share (%) ÷ Weighted Distribution. Measures how much value share a brand extracts per point of distribution. Higher = stronger brand pull or pricing power. Efficiency >15 = under-distributed (room to grow); <10 = over-distributed (diminishing returns).
[6]Vol% / Val% — Brand's share of total category volume or value in the measured period (February 2026). Volume in K Litres, Value in Mn IQD.

Price Tiers

[7]Value — PPV below 4,000 IQD/L. Dominated by local/Turkish brands (IPEK, HAIR, DURU). High volume, low margin.
[8]Economy — PPV 4,000–7,500 IQD/L. Mass-market sweet spot (COSMAL CURE, TRESEMME, LIFEBUOY). Dabur share: 0.4%.
[9]Mainstream — PPV 7,500–11,000 IQD/L. Vatika plays here (PPV 8,810). Also GARNIER, PANTENE. Dabur share: 4.3%.
[10]Premium — PPV 11,000–15,000 IQD/L. Largest value pool (32% of market value). HEAD&SHOULDERS, CLEAR dominate. Dabur share: 0%.
[11]Super-Premium — PPV above 15,000 IQD/L. Niche segment (3.5% vol, 9.2% val). Salon and specialist brands.

Geography & Channels

[12]Perfumery — Specialist beauty/personal care retail outlets. Largest channel (47% of market volume). Dabur's core channel (WD 16.8).
[13]Modern Trade — Supermarkets, hypermarkets, organised retail chains. 15% of volume. Higher per-store throughput.
[14]Traditional Trade — Independent grocers, neighbourhood shops. 37% of volume but Vatika WD just 1.2 — largest untapped channel.
[15]Regions — Iraq is divided into 5 audit regions: Baghdad (capital, largest), Kurdistan (autonomous north), North (Nineveh/Kirkuk), South (Basra/Maysan), Furat (mid-Euphrates).

Origin Classification

[16]MNC — Multinational Corporation (P&G, Unilever, L'Oréal). 69% of market value. Turkish — IPEK, DURU. Iraqi — HAIR, ASSAYEL, local brands. Lebanese — COSMAL CURE, SOFT WAVE. Indian — VATIKA, AMLA (Dabur).
